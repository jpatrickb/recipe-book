Alfredo Sauce

  • 1 c heavy cream
  • 1 cube butter
  • 1 cup Parmesan cheese
  • Minced garlic or garlic salt

Over medium heat, bring cream and butter to a boil. Remove from heat, add cheese and stir to melt. Season with salt & pepper, garlic if desired. Serve over 16 oz cooked pasta.

Baked Fried Chicken

  • 2-3 lbs chicken tenders
  • bowl of milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp seasoned salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 c flour
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 c butter

Preheat oven to 400. Place chicken strips in a bowl and cover with milk; let soak 20-30 min. Mix dry ingredients in a ziploc bag. Cut the butter into pieces and put in a 9x13 pan. Put in oven until melted.

Shake excess milk off chicken and coat each piece with the dry mix. Place in pan, bake 40 min, turning halfway through.

Baked Ham Sandwiches

  • 12 sweet hawaiian dinner rolls, halved
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 3/4 pound thinly sliced ham
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 6 slices cheese
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 4 Tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp poppy seed

Place the bottoms of the rolls in a baking dish. Top with the ham and cheese, cut to size if necessary. Melt the butter and add the rest of the ingredients. Brush the butter mixture over the sandwiches, using it all, then cover with foil. Chill several hours. Bake at 375 for 15 minutes, removing the foil for the last few minutes.

Baked Macaroni & Cheese

  • 1/2 pound macaroni
  • 3 Tbsp butter
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • 2 1/2 c milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 pounds cheddar cheese, cubed
  • 2 Tbsp parmesan cheese
  • Bread crumbs

Heat oven to 375. Grease 2 qt. baking dish. Cook macaroni according to package directions; drain and rinse with cold water. Melt ~ 1 Tbsp butter over low heat & mix in flour. Cook & stir about 1 minute and gradually stir in milk. Add salt & pepper and bring to a simmer. Simmer 3 minutes, stirring frequently. Add cheese cubes and stir until partially melted. Remove from heat and add macaroni adn 2 Tbsp Parmesan cheese and stir. Pour into baking dish and top with buttered bread crumbs mixed with some Parmesan. Bake 25-30 minutes or until crumbs are browned and mac is bubbly at edges.

Balsamic Chicken Sandwiches

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 bottle balsamic vinaigrette
  • 6 sub sandwich buns, ciabatta rolls, or 2 baguettes
  • 1 lb fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced
  • 6 Tbsp pesto
  • 3 tomatoes, thinly sliced

Marinate chicken in vinaigrette overnight. Grill chicken until cooked through. Slice chicken into thin slices.

If using baguettes, cut each into 3 pieces, then slice open. Spread one side of bread with 1 tbsp pesto. Layer chicken, cheese, and tomatoes, topping with other side of bread. Serve immediately or wrap in foil and heat 5-10 minutes in 300-degree oven. Serves 6.

Beef Fajitas

  • 1 lb beef strips
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2-3 peppers, sliced (any color bell peppers)
  • 2 tomatoes, cut into thin wedges
  • 1 package taco seasoning or fajita seasoning
  • 1 package tortillas
  • Sour cream
  • Salsa
  • Shredded cheese

In a skillet over med-hi heat, cook meat and onion in 2 Tbsp oil until meat is all brown. Add peppers and cook several more minutes until crisp-tender. Add tomatoes and taco seasoning combined with 1/4 c water and cook just until water is gone. Serve on tortillas, and top with sour cream, salsa, and cheese.

Beef Stroganoff

  • 8 oz pasta
  • 1 lb beef, cubed
  • 1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 c sour cream
  • 1-2 beef boullion cubes
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 c milk
  • Salt & pepper

Prepare pasta according to package directions. Meanwhile, heat 1 Tbsp oil in a large skillet or saucepan and cook beef until no longer pink. In a mixing bowl, combine cream soup, sour cream, milk, and Worcestershire sauce. Whisk until well combined. Add along with bouillon cube(s) to meat and cook over med-lo heat, stirring occasionally, until warm and bubbly. Make sure bouillon cubes are dissolved and mixed in. Season with salt and pepper. When pasta is done, serve sauce over pasta. Serves 4-6.

Browned Butter with Mizithra Cheese Pasta

  • 8 ounce spaghetti noodles
  • 1/2 c butter
  • 1/2 c mizithra cheese, grated
  • Chopped parsley for garnish (optional)

Cook the spaghetti according to package directions until soft. While noodles are cooking, brown the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. The butter will turn a yellow, start to froth and then become brown. Drain the pasta but do not rinse. Pour the brown butter over the pasta. Sprinkle with mizithra cheese and toss.

Butternut Squash Risotto

  • 6 cups Chicken (or vegetable) broth
  • Butternut squash
  • 3 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 Tbsp sage
  • 1 Tbsp rosemary
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese

Heat the broth: Pour 6 cups of broth into a medium pot and set it over medium heat. Bring it to a low simmer, then reduce the heat and cover the pot to keep it warm.

Prep the squash: Use a sharp, sturdy knife to trim off the ends. Cut the squash in half so that you have the bulbous end and the neck. Working with one section at a time, use the knife or a Y-shaped vegetable peeler to remove the tough outer skin of the squash. Cut each section in half and use a spoon to scoop out the seeds from the bulbous end. Cut the peel squash into 3/4-inch cubes.

Saute the onion: Into a large deep skillet, 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil and add the onion. Cook on medium-low until the onion is soft and a pale golden color. This will take about 8 minutes. Add the squash chunks and stir to coat them with oil. Cook for about 10 minutes, until the squash is beginning to soften but is still quite firm. Season with 1/2 teaspoon salt, a grinding of black pepper, and 1 tablespoon each of finely chopped rosemary and sage--about 2 teaspoons each. Cook until the squash is just tender, about 4 minutes longer. Then scoop out half the pieces of squash and set them aside in a bowl. These will be added back later.

Add the rice. Stir in 1 1/2 cups of rice, and "toast" it, coating it well with oil and stirring for 3 to 5 minutes, until it is pearly and translucent, and you start to hear it crackle. If the pot seems dry, add another tablespoon of olive oil. Season with 1/2 teaspoon salt. Ladle in 3/4 to 1 cup of hot broth, stirring with a sturdy hand and a circular motion. Keep stirring until the broth is completely absorbed. Continue to add broth by the ladleful, stirring regularly and allowing the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more.

Add the reserved squash. After 15 minutes of cooking the rice, stir in the reserved pieces of squash. Continue to cook the risotto, adding broth as needed, until the risotto is creamy and al dente. Taste a small spoonful; the grains should be tender with just a tiny bit of firmness at the center, but no chalkiness. If the rice is chalky, add another ladleful of broth and stir until the rice is creamy and al dente. Add a final splash of broth to give the risotto its signature "wavy" or "flowing" consistency; it should be slightly runny but still spoonable.

Finish the risotto and serve. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in a handful--about 1/2 cup--of Parmesan cheese and a knob of butter (optional), about 1 tablespoon. Stir vigorously until the cheese and butter are fully incorporated.

Calzone

  • Dough for 1 loaf of bread
  • 1 can spaghetti sauce
  • 1 lb grated mozzarella cheese
  • Meat (pepperoni, sausage, ham, ground beef, etc.)

Preheat oven to 375. Dive dough into 4 pieces. Roll each piece into a circle about 10 inches in diameter. Fill with meat, cheese, and spaghetti sauce. Fold in half and pinch edges closed. Place on a greased baking sheet. Bake for about 20 minutes or until golden brown.

Chicken Alfredo

  • 4 oz cream cheese
  • 1 1/2 c milk
  • 3/4 c grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 can chicken breast meat
  • 1 c frozen peas
  • 8 oz cooked fettuccine

Heat the cream cheese & milk over medium heat to a boil, stirring constantly. Stir in 1/2 c Parmesan cheese. Reduce heat to low and cook 5 min. Add the chicken, peas, & pasta to pot. Top with remaining cheese & serve.

For a more traditional sauce, substitute 2 Tbsp butter & 1 1/2 c heavy cream for cream cheese & milk.

Chicken Burritos

  • 1 green pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 lbs chicken breast, cubed
  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup enchilada sauce or other hot sauce (type and quantity dependent on preference)
  • 1 can whole kernel corn
  • 1 can black beans
  • 12 tortillas

Dice the pepper and onion, and saute them in a pan until just before they begin to caramelize. Then add the chicken and cook thoroughly. Add cream cheese, enchilada or hot sauce, corn, beans, and stir until well-mixed. Add to tortillas and serve.

Chicken Enchiladas

  • 12 medium flour tortillas
  • 1 can chicken, reserving broth
  • 1 can black or kidney beans, drained
  • 1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 8 oz sour cream
  • 1/4-1/2 c milk
  • 2 c grated cheddar cheese
  • Salt & pepper

Grease a 9x13 baking dish. Preheat oven to 350.

In a mixing bowl, combine the cream soup, sour cream, broth from the canned chicken, and enough milk to make a creamy sauce. In another mixing bowl, break the chicken into pieces, then stir in the beans. Divide the meat/bean mix among the 12 tortillas, topping the meat with 1/4 c cheese & 2 Tbsp or so of sauce. Roll the tortillas up and place them side by side in the baking dish. Top with remaining sauce, making sure to cover all the tortillas. Top with remaining cheese. Bake 20/30 minutes, or until heated through. Serve with chopped olives, salsa, and guacamole.

Chicken Pillows

  • 1 c cubed chicken (canned works fine)
  • 4 oz cream cheese
  • 2 Tbsp fresh chives
  • Garlic salt and/or salt & pepper
  • 1 pkg refrigerated crescent rolls
  • 4 Tbsp melted butter
  • 1/2 c bread crumbs
  • 1 pkg chicken gravy mix

Combine first 4 ingredients & stir just until mixed. Roll out crescent rolls, keeping 2 together to form a rectangle rather than a triangle. Put 1/4 of filling in each roll, fold over & seal. Dip in melted butter, then breadcrumbs. Bake at 375 for 20 minutes or until golden brown. Serve topped with chicken gravy. Serves 2-4.

Philly Cheesesteak Sloppy Joe's

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 8 oz mushrooms, minced
  • 2 Tbsp ketchup
  • 1 Tbsp worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 Tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 c beef broth
  • Sliced provolone cheese
  • 8 hamburger buns

In a large cast iron skillet flatten the ground beef, then cook over medium heat without stirring until bottom side is brown and crispy. Flip over and continue cooking, stirring and breaking up the meat, until no pink remains. Remove meat from pan. In the meat drippings (drain if there's too much) cook the vegetables for a few minutes until soft. Meanwhile, dissolve the cornstarch in the broth, then add the ketchup, worcestershire sauce and salt & pepper.

Return the meat to the pan and add the broth mixture. Simmer over low heat for 5 minutes or until little liquid remains. Toast buns until golden, then top with meat mixture and a slice of cheese. Return to broiler if desired to melt cheese.

Turkish Flatbread (Naan Bread)

  • 1 1/4 cup warm water
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 3/4 cup plain greek yogurt
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp salt
  • 3 3/4 cups flour
  • 1/4 cup parsley

Combine the yeast, sugar and water in a large bowl and stir well. Allow to sit 5-10 minutes. Whisk in the greek yogurt, olive oil and salt. Add flour and stir until the dough comes together, adding a bit of flour if necessary. Knead 3-4 minutes. Divide into 20 equal pieces and cover with a kitchen towel. Allow to rest for 15 minutes.

Preheat a medium saucepan to medium/low heat. Roll the dough into 7 inch circles, lightly brushing the top with olive oil. When the pan is hot, place the dough, oiled side down, on the pan. Lightly brush top with oil. Cook 1-2 minutes until the top surface is covered with bubbles and underside is golden in spots.

Flip and cook until the other side has golden brown spots. Place on a kitchen towel spread over a plate, sprinkle with parsley, and cover to keep moist.

Desserts

French Silk Pie

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup butter (not margarine)
  • 3 squares (3 ounces) unsweetened chocolate, melted and cooled
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 3 eggs
  • Baked pastry shell

In a bowl beat sugar and butter with an electric mixer on medium speed about 4 minutes or till fluffy. Stir in chocolate and vanilla. Add eggs, one at a time, beating on high speed after each addition and scraping sides of bowl constantly. Transfer to baked pastry shell. Cover; chill for 5-24 hours or till set. If desired, top with whipped cream and chocolate curls.

Fresh Peach Pie

  • 8 large fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 9 inch graham cracker pie crust
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

In a small sauce pan, mix sugar, water and cornstarch until completely dissolved. Heat mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until mixture boils and thickens. Remove from heat and cool completely. Glaze will thicken more as it cools.

Place peeled, sliced peaches in large bowl. Pour half of the glaze over the fruit. Gently mix to coat all the peaches. Add additional glaze if necessary.

Pour glazed peaches into prepared pie crust. Pile peaches as high as desired. Refrigerate for several hours or overnight to set. Serve cold with whipped cream.

Ginger Snaps

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Cream butter and sugar. Add egg and molasses. Add dry ingredients and mix well. Form into small balls. Roll in sugar. Bake for 10 minutes. Cool on rack.

Melt butter and baking chocolate. Add evaporated milk, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Boil, stirring constantly, 6 min or until it starts to thicken.

Lemon Bars

  • Crust
    • 3/4 cup melted butter
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 2 tsp vanilla
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • Filling
    • 2 cups sugar
    • 6 Tbsp flour
    • 6 large eggs
    • 1 cup lemon juice
    • 1/2 cup powdered sugar

In a medium bowl, mix melted butter with sugar, vanilla and salt. Add flour and blend. Dough will be thick like cookie dough. Press dough in the bottom of a 9x9 glass baking pan. Bake at 350 degrees for 20 minutes or until edges begin to brown slightly.

In a mixing bowl, whisk eggs, sugar and flour until thick and light yellow in color. Add lemon juice slowly, whisking as you pour. Pour lemon filling over crust and return to oven. Bake for about 25 minutes or until your bars are completely set. Remove from oven, and allow to cool. Refrigerate bars for at least 2 hours or until chilled.

Peanut Butter Fudge

  • 8 oz butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 c smooth peanut butter
  • 1 lb powdered sugar

Combine the butter and peanut butter in a saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until melted. Add the vanilla and sugar and stir to combine. Spread into a buttered 8x8 pan lined with parchment paper. Fold the excess parchment paper to cover the surface of the fudge and refrigerate until cool. Cut into 1 pieces and store in an airtight container at room temp for up to a week.

Shannon's Raspberry Cake

  • 1 white cake mix
  • 1 12 oz bag frozen raspberries
  • 1 box Danish Dessert (raspberry)
  • 1 16 oz carton of cool whip
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 8 oz packages of cream cheese

Bake white cake on sheet cake pan for 20 minutes. Cool.
Drain juice from thawed raspberries and add enough water to equal 1 1/3 cup of raspberry water mixture.
Mix juice and water with raspberry Danish Dessert and microwave until thick (3-5 minutes). Add raspberries and let cool.
Whip 1 cup powdered sugar with softened cream cheese.
Add cool whip to cheese mixture and spread on cooled cake.
Spread raspberry mixture on top. Chill and serve.
